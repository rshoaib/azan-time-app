About Prayer Times in the UK

The UK sits at a high latitude (51–54°N), which means during summer the sun never dips far enough below the horizon for a true astronomical Fajr or Isha. Most British mosques use the Muslim World League (MWL) method with a "middle of the night" or "one-seventh of the night" approximation for Isha during those months — Azan Time applies the same rule automatically.

Times switch between GMT (winter) and BST (summer) automatically. The Qibla from the UK points roughly southeast — about 119° from London. Asr typically follows the Shafi'i calculation, though some Deobandi-affiliated mosques use the Hanafi rule (Azan Time lets you choose).
