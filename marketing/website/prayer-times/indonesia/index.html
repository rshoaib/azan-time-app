About Prayer Times in Indonesia

Indonesia — home to the world's largest Muslim population — predominantly uses the Muslim World League (MWL) method with Fajr at 18° and Isha at 17°, matching the standard published by Kementerian Agama (Kemenag). Asr follows the Shafi'i calculation (shadow = object length), consistent with the dominant madhhab in the region.

The Qibla from Indonesia points roughly northwest — about 292° from Jakarta. During Ramadan, sahur ends at Fajr (Imsak is traditionally called 10 minutes earlier) and buka puasa begins at Maghrib. Azan Time shows live countdowns for both.
