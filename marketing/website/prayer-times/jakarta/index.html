About Prayer Times in Jakarta

Muslims in Jakarta observe five daily prayers: Fajr before sunrise, Dhuhr after midday, Asr in the afternoon, Maghrib at sunset, and Isha at night. The exact times vary slightly throughout the year based on the sun's position and the calculation method used by your local masjid.

Azan Time supports 8 calculation methods commonly used across the Muslim world, including the Muslim World League standard (default in Indonesia and most global locations), ISNA (common in North America), Umm al-Qura (Saudi Arabia), and Karachi (common in South Asia).

During Ramadan, the app also shows precise Suhoor and Iftar times for Jakarta, with live countdowns so you never miss sahur or rush for iftar.
