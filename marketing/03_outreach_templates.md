# Outreach Templates — Copy-Paste Ready

Every template is short. Short templates get replies. Long pitches get ignored.

---

## 1. Creator Outreach (Instagram / TikTok / YouTube DMs)

**Subject line (email):** `A small prayer app we'd love your thoughts on`

**Message:**

> Assalamu alaikum [First name],
>
> I came across your content on [platform] — your [specific post / reel / video] on [topic] really stuck with me. JazakAllah khair for the work you do.
>
> I'm [Your name], and I built Azan Time — a prayer app that keeps things simple: accurate salah times, authentic adhan, Qibla compass that works offline, and a streak tracker that helps build the habit. No paid subscription, just the app.
>
> No pitch — honestly, I'd just love for you to try it. If it's useful, and only if it's useful, I'd be honored if you shared it with your audience. If not, inshaAllah your feedback would mean everything.
>
> iOS: [link]
> Android: [link]
>
> Either way, thank you for the content. May Allah reward you.
>
> — [Your name]

**Response rate:** Expect ~5–10%. Follow up once after 7 days, never twice.

**Who to send to:**
- Instagram: search `islamicreminders`, `dailyhadith`, `muslimlife`, `quranquotes` — prioritize 10k–500k followers
- TikTok: search `#muslim`, `#ramadan`, `#islamicreminder` — filter creators 20k–1M
- YouTube: smaller English-speaking Muslim channels (khutbah clips, "day in the life of a Muslim", revert stories)

**Do NOT** send to: top-tier scholars, controversial/political accounts, anyone with paid partnership disclosures on every post.

## 2. Masjid Admin Outreach (WhatsApp / Email)

**Channel:** WhatsApp is best. Find the masjid's public number via Google Maps or their website.

**Message:**

> Assalamu alaikum, brother/sister.
>
> I'm a Muslim developer reaching out to masjids I admire. I built a prayer app — Azan Time — that's been used by a few thousand people so far, and feedback has been positive, alhamdulillah.
>
> If you think your congregation would benefit, I'd love to send you a printable flyer (PDF, one page) you could share in your WhatsApp group or pin on the masjid noticeboard. No ask, no fee — I just want more brothers and sisters to have a reliable prayer app.
>
> If you'd like the flyer, just reply "yes" and I'll send it over.
>
> JazakAllah khair for everything your masjid does.

**Follow-up after they say yes:** Attach the PDF (`marketing/masjid_flyer.html` rendered to PDF) + a short thank-you message.

## 3. Imam / Scholar Outreach (personal, longer)

**Only send to scholars whose work you genuinely follow.** This is not a volume play.

**Email subject:** `[Your first name] — thank you, and a small question`

> Assalamu alaikum Shaykh [Last name],
>
> I've benefited from your [specific lecture / book / series on a particular topic] — specifically [one-line of what you learned]. JazakAllah khair for the work; may Allah accept it.
>
> I'm writing for one small reason: I built an app called Azan Time that helps Muslims keep to their salah times, find Qibla, and build consistency in prayer. I'd be honored if you tried it, and if it aligns with how you see technology serving the ummah, would value any guidance you have.
>
> No ask beyond your opinion. If you ever felt inclined to mention it to your students, that would be a barakah I could never repay — but it's not what I'm asking for.
>
> iOS: [link]
> Android: [link]
>
> May Allah preserve you.

**Response rate:** <5%. But one yes from the right scholar = 10,000+ qualified installs.

## 4. Community Group Outreach (Facebook / Telegram / Discord)

**DO NOT** spam. The rule: contribute first, mention second.

**Approach:** Join 5–10 large Muslim Facebook / Telegram / Discord groups. Comment helpfully on 10+ other posts over 2 weeks. THEN make one post about your app, with this framing:

> Salaams everyone — I wanted to share something I built that some of you might find useful.
>
> I kept missing Fajr and got tired of the prayer apps full of ads, so I built Azan Time. It's free, no subscription, works offline for Qibla, and has a streak tracker for building consistency.
>
> If any of you try it, I'd love your brutally honest feedback. The app is only as good as the ummah tells me it is.
>
> iOS: [link]
> Android: [link]
>
> JazakAllah khair.

**Tips:**
- Always disclose it's your app
- Reply to every comment personally
- Never post twice in the same group

## 5. Reply Templates — App Store & Play Store Reviews

**Reply to EVERY review.** Stores factor developer response rate into ranking. Negative reviewers who get a thoughtful reply often update to 4–5 stars.

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Positive review
> JazakAllah khair for the kind words and for using Azan Time. May Allah accept your salah. If there's ever a feature you'd love to see, I personally read every email at support@azantime.app.

### ⭐⭐⭐ Mixed review
> Thank you for the honest feedback — it's exactly what helps the app improve. You mentioned [quote their specific issue]. We've noted this and [state what you're doing about it, even if it's "looking into it for the next release"]. If you'd email support@azantime.app I'd be glad to follow up personally.

### ⭐⭐ Negative review — legitimate issue
> I'm truly sorry this happened, and JazakAllah khair for taking the time to write. [Acknowledge the specific issue]. This is being fixed in the next release (ETA: [date or "this month"]). If you email support@azantime.app I'll let you know personally when the fix ships — and I'd be grateful if you'd consider updating the review then.

### ⭐ 1-star — vague/troll review
> JazakAllah khair for trying the app. If there's something specific you'd like to see improved, I'd value the feedback at support@azantime.app. May Allah bless you.

### ⭐ 1-star — incorrect prayer times claim
> I'm sorry the times didn't match what you expected. Azan Time supports 8 calculation methods (Muslim World League, ISNA, Umm al-Qura, Karachi, Dubai, Qatar, Kuwait, Egyptian General Authority) — you may need to select the one your local masjid uses in Settings → Prayer Calculation. If you'd email support@azantime.app with your city, I'd personally verify the times for you.

## 6. Press / Blog Outreach

Only 2–3 outlets matter for prayer apps:
- **AboutIslam.net**
- **IslamicFinder blog** (competitor, but they sometimes link out)
- **Productivity Islamic bloggers** (Mufti Menk, Nouman Ali Khan accounts — but only if you get a referral)

**Pitch (email):**

> Hi [Editor name],
>
> Quick pitch: I built Azan Time, a prayer app that's gotten [X downloads / 4.7 stars / specific traction point] since launch — without any ads or subscription.
>
> I think your readers would find it useful, especially [the one feature most relevant to their audience]. Happy to provide screenshots, a review build, or jump on a 15-min call.
>
> Here's a quick 1-page product brief: [link to one-pager]
>
> JazakAllah khair for considering.
> [Your name + link to LinkedIn or portfolio]

**Don't:** pitch generic tech blogs (TechCrunch etc.) — they won't cover a prayer app unless you have $1M+ ARR or a major funding announcement.

## 7. Subreddit Posts — r/islam, r/MuslimLounge, etc.

**Rule #1:** Build karma FIRST. Comment helpfully on 15+ other threads before posting your own.
**Rule #2:** One post per subreddit, maximum, every 60 days.
**Rule #3:** Flair the post as `Self-promotion` or whatever the sub requires.

**r/islam — Q&A framing:**

> **Title:** How do you remember to pray Fajr? I built a small app — would love feedback from this community.
>
> Salaams everyone. I'm [name], a Muslim developer, and I kept struggling with Fajr. So I built Azan Time — a free prayer app with accurate times, authentic adhan, offline Qibla, and a streak tracker.
>
> It's on iOS and Android. No ads between screens, no subscription.
>
> Would really value this community's feedback — both what you like and what you'd want added. I read every comment.
>
> iOS: [link]
> Android: [link]
>
> (Mods, please remove if this isn't allowed.)

**r/indonesia / r/pakistan / r/bangladesh — localized framing:**

> **Title:** Aplikasi waktu sholat gratis yang saya bangun (prayer times app I built)
> — [Write 3–4 sentences in local language], app link, then English translation below.

Post in local language first; it massively outperforms English-only posts in these subs.

## 8. Twitter / X Posts

You don't need followers. You need 2 things: the right hashtag, the right time.

**Best times:** Friday morning (Jumu'ah), right before Maghrib, during the first 10 nights of Ramadan.

**Post template (evergreen):**

> I built this because I kept missing Fajr.
>
> Azan Time — free prayer app. Accurate times, real adhan, offline Qibla, streak tracker. No ads, no subscription.
>
> [1-2 screenshots]
>
> iOS: [link]
> Android: [link]

**Post template (Ramadan — peak season):**

> Ramadan Mubarak 🌙
>
> I spent the last year building a prayer app that respects your attention. Suhoor & Iftar countdowns, accurate salah times, offline Qibla, and a streak tracker to build consistency.
>
> Free, forever. No subscription.
>
> [link]

**Hashtags that actually matter:** `#Ramadan`, `#RamadanMubarak`, `#Muslim`, `#Islam`, `#Eid` (seasonally). Avoid spammy tags like `#MuslimApp` — they're dead.

## Tracking & Cadence

| Channel | How many per week | Track what |
|---|---|---|
| Creator DMs | 10–15 | Reply rate, mentions |
| Masjid WhatsApp | 5–10 | Which masjids said yes |
| Subreddit posts | 1 per sub, per quarter | Upvotes, comments, install spike |
| Review replies | ALL of them, within 72h | Review-update rate after reply |
| Twitter/X | 3–4 per week | Link clicks (use a URL with UTM) |

Use a free Airtable or Notion template to track who you contacted and when — don't rely on memory.
