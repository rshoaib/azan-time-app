About Prayer Times in the USA

Most American mosques use the ISNA (Islamic Society of North America) method — Fajr at 15° and Isha at 15° below the horizon. This produces times that are slightly later for Fajr and earlier for Isha compared to the Muslim World League standard, which many communities find more practical for North American schedules and latitudes.

The US spans four time zones (ET, CT, MT, PT) plus Alaska and Hawaii — Azan Time detects your zone from GPS and applies DST changes automatically in March and November. The Qibla from the US points northeast: roughly 58° from New York, 47° from Chicago, and 24° from Los Angeles.

Asr follows the Shafi'i calculation by default but can be switched to Hanafi for South Asian-heritage communities. Select a city above for today's times.
