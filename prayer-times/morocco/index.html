About Prayer Times in Morocco

Morocco predominantly follows the Maliki madhhab and uses the Ministry of Habous and Islamic Affairs calculation — Fajr at 18° and Isha at 17° below the horizon. Asr follows the Shafi'i / Maliki standard (shadow = object length + midday shadow). Times are also available via the Muslim World League method which produces nearly identical results.

Morocco observes permanent UTC+1 (GMT+1 year-round), having dropped seasonal DST in 2018. During Ramadan, however, the country temporarily reverts to GMT for the holy month only. Azan Time handles this edge case automatically.

The Qibla from Morocco points roughly east-southeast — about 91° from Casablanca, 96° from Marrakech. Install Azan Time for live adhan, offline Qibla and a Hijri calendar — entièrement gratuit.
